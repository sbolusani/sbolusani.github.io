Research Overview

My research focuses on integer optimization, with an emphasis on mixed-integer linear optimization (MILP) and its extensions to bilevel, multilevel, and multistage problems. I work on both algorithmic development and solver implementation, with an interest in how theoretical ideas behave when implemented in optimization software. A primary motivation in my work is to understand better—and gradually narrow—the gap between optimization theory and computational practice.

Mixed-Integer Linear Optimization and Warm Starting

A recurring theme in my work is warm starting, or reoptimization, of MILPs. Warm starting arises in many contexts: real-world applications where similar problems are solved repeatedly with small data changes (such as security-constrained unit commitment), iterative algorithms like Benders’ or Dantzig–Wolfe decomposition that generate closely related subproblems, and scenario-based stochastic optimization where problem instances differ only slightly across scenarios.

My work in this area includes implementing solver components within branch-and-bound frameworks such as SYMPHONY and developing tools and benchmarking datasets to study warm-starting behavior in a controlled, reproducible manner. Some of these datasets were used in The MIP Computational Competition 2023, which focused on reoptimization of MILPs.

Multilevel and Multistage Mixed-Integer Linear Optimization

The central theme of my Ph.D. work is the study of parametric valid inequalities (PVIs) for MILPs, particularly inequalities derived from duality and the value function of MILPs. These PVIs are applied to multilevel and multistage MILPs, with a focus on bilevel and two-stage settings. Using PVIs, I developed an abstract framework that generalizes classical Benders’ decomposition to these problem classes. This framework yields a convergent generalized Benders’ decomposition algorithm capable of handling generic MILPs as subproblems.

I have also developed a new open-source solver that implements this generalized Benders’ decomposition algorithm for multilevel and multistage problems. SYMPHONY is used as the subproblem solver, both for its dual-function data capabilities and warm-start support. The same PVIs naturally relate to warm starting of MILPs, allowing information from related solves to be reused across problem instances. A summary of this work is available in this poster.

Solver Development and Software

A substantial part of my work involves implementing algorithms in open-source optimization solvers. I have contributed to SCIP, SYMPHONY, MibS, and MINOTAUR, primarily in C and C++, with supporting tools and interfaces in Python, such as PySoPlex.

My contributions span:

  • Solver components: cutting planes, primal heuristics, and new branching strategies
  • Algorithmic prototypes: new decomposition and warm-starting methods
  • Infrastructure: testing, benchmarking, and integration pipelines

Applications and Earlier Work

These methods and solvers have been applied to practical problems such as security-constrained unit commitment, automated timetabling, and stock layout planning. Earlier work also includes branching strategies for convex mixed-integer nonlinear optimization developed during my Master’s studies, and implemented and evaluated within MINOTAUR.
