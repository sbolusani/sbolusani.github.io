Suresh Bolusani

Researcher and Solver Developer in Mathematical Optimization

I work on integer optimization, primarily mixed-integer linear optimization and its extensions to bilevel, multilevel, and multistage settings. I am interested in developing theory and algorithms for these problem classes, and understanding the algorithmic behavior upon implementation in actual solvers, with a focus on which theoretically sound ideas translate well into practice and why. A central theme of my work is trying to narrow the gap between optimization theory and practice.

From 2021 to 2025, I worked as a researcher at Zuse Institute Berlin in the Mathematical Optimization Methods group, where I focused on mixed-integer linear optimization, both solving them from scratch and warm-starting them by transferring information from previous solves of similar problems. During this period, I contributed algorithmic components and computational tools to open-source solvers, most notably SCIP.

My Ph.D. work at Lehigh University focuses on parametric valid inequalities and decomposition methods for multilevel and multistage mixed-integer linear optimization problems, with an emphasis on two levels and two stages. This work includes the development of generalized Benders' decomposition framework and algorithms for these problem classes, warm starting of mixed-integer linear optimization problems, and corresponding solver implementations intended to support further research.

Across my academic and professional work, I have contributed to multiple open-source solvers such as SCIP, SYMPHONY, MibS, and MINOTAUR, primarily in C and C++, with supporting tools and interfaces in Python, such as PySoPlex. I also have experience with commercial solvers and modeling languages, including CPLEX, Gurobi, and AMPL. These experiences cover algorithmic components, solver integration, and computational benchmarking.
